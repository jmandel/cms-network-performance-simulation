Four Architectures

BrokerSubscriptions Broker

One subscription per client at the Broker. Networks route everything. Sources have one connection.

DirectPer-Patient Source Feed

Clients subscribe directly at each source. Networks only signal new care relationships.

Direct+GroupSource Feed + Group Subs

Like Direct, but payers use one Group per source (DaVinci ATR) instead of per-patient subscriptions.

EncryptedEncrypted Relay

Broker routing with end-to-end encryption. Networks relay without reading content.

Total Work: Direct Models Do Less

Direct models generate ~34% fewer total messages than Broker. This is not zero-sum — Broker's relay chain (source→network→peer networks→clients) creates intermediate hops that are genuinely eliminated when clients subscribe directly at sources. The end delivery count (notifications received by apps, payers, providers) is the same across all models.

Why the difference? Every clinical event in Broker traverses source→network→peer network→client, generating total messages. In Direct, the source sends straight to subscribers — total messages. The saved messages are relay hops that carried duplicated data through network infrastructure. Both Direct variants are identical here — Group subscriptions don't change notification delivery, only subscription management.

Who Does the Work: Sources vs Networks

The total work reduction comes with a shift in who bears the load. Broker concentrates work at networks (purpose-built infrastructure). Direct distributes it to sources (provider EHRs that may lack subscription capabilities today).

Adoption question: Direct requires a p95 source to manage subscriptions and send msgs/day. Direct+Group reduces subscriptions to (by collapsing payer per-patient subs into Group subs) but source outbound messages stay the same. Broker requires zero subscription management at sources — they send msgs/day to their network and nothing else.

Payers: The Biggest Winners and Losers

Payers drive the majority of subscription state in Direct. With 90% coverage and ~1.28 payers per patient, payer subscriptions account for 62% of Direct's source subscription burden. This is where the architectural choice matters most.

Direct+Group: Source Sub Breakdown

Ghost subscription risk (Direct only): When a member changes plans, subscriptions must be deactivated at every source the patient visited. At 5% failure rate, ghost subscriptions accumulate, generating wasted notifications/year — clinical data sent to payers who no longer cover the patient.
Direct+Group changes the game for payers: Payer subs collapse from 959M per-patient subscriptions to ~180K long-lived Group subs. Member churn becomes Group membership operations, not subscription CRUD. The total work payers do is comparable to Broker, while preserving Direct's lower total message volume.

Tradeoff Summary

No architecture dominates all dimensions. Direct models reduce total system work but shift burden to sources. Direct+Group reclaims most of the payer efficiency of Broker while keeping Direct's message savings. Encrypted adds privacy at the cost of the highest total work.

Scenario sensitivity

These conclusions are stable across scenarios (mature adoption, high fragmentation, cross-network mix, high payer churn). The relative ordering does not change. See the Explorer to test specific parameter combinations.

Methodology

A TypeScript simulator generates a synthetic U.S.-scale ecosystem and counts every message, subscription, and state record for each architecture. It is a decision-support model, not a prediction engine.
